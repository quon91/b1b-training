VOR Approach Guide

VOR station tracking using FMS NAV page tuning, NAV1 PFD mode, and NAV HOLD autopilot. Vertical guidance managed manually via step-down altitudes to MDA. No glideslope exists.

Phase 1 — VOR tuning and PFD setup
1
FMS NAV PAGE — tune VOR frequency and course
FMS — press NAV key on CDU
RADIO NAV VOR1/FREQ CRS [ABI]/113.7 359 VOR2/FREQ CRS [ ]/[ ] --- ADF1/FREQ BRG [ ]/890 --- TAC1/FREQ CRS [ ]/[ ] ---
  • Press NAV key on FMS CDU
  • Type VOR frequency from approach plate (e.g. Abilene VOR: 113.7)
  • Press left key → VOR1 to assign it
  • Confirm identifier shows station ID (e.g. ABI)
  • Type inbound course from plate (e.g. 359)
  • Press right key → CRS
  • VOR1 DME distance will display on PFD
2
PFD — switch nav mode to NAV1
PFD lower softkeys
VSD | ADF | ILS | [NAV1] | GPS ↑ press this
AP ALT HDG NAV ATH APR CRS 359 VOR1 DME ABI 24.6
  • Press the NAV1 softkey at the bottom of the PFD
  • Compass rose now shows the VOR course deviation needle
  • VOR1 identifier and DME distance appear top-right of PFD
  • Needle shows deviation from the inbound course you set
In NAV1 mode the NAV HOLD autopilot tracks the VOR course needle — not the FMS GPS path. Make sure VOR is in range before switching to NAV HOLD.
3
CRS selector — set OBS course on autopilot panel
  • Rotate the CRS selector knob (lower center panel — item 5) to your inbound course
  • This sets the course reference for NAV HOLD tracking
  • Must match the CRS entered in FMS NAV page
  • Example for VOR RWY 34L: set CRS selector to 359
If the CRS selector does not match your FMS NAV page CRS setting, the autopilot NAV HOLD may track the wrong radial. Verify both are set to the same inbound course.
4
Read the approach plate — key items for VOR
  • VOR frequency — enter in FMS NAV page VOR1 field
  • VOR identifier — confirm it appears after tuning
  • Inbound course — enter in CRS field and CRS selector knob
  • IAF, step-down fixes, FAF — note all altitudes at each fix
  • MDA — enter in FMS APPR PAGE DH field
  • MAP location — know the missed approach point before starting
  • Missed approach procedure — brief the heading, altitude, and fix

After intercept — NAV HOLD tracks VOR radial
  • When VOR needle begins moving toward center: press NAV HOLD
  • HDG drops out — aircraft tracks VOR course automatically
  • Never use APR mode for VOR approaches
2
VOR station passage — needle reverses
  • When you fly directly over the VOR station the needle will swing rapidly from one side to the other — this is called station passage
  • Do not chase the needle during passage — hold your heading
  • After passage the needle resettles on the outbound course
  • Some VOR approaches use the station as the FAF — begin descent at station passage
  • Others have a fix beyond the station — continue to that fix before descending
Brief the approach plate so you know whether station passage or a subsequent fix is your descent point. Getting this wrong causes either an early descent or a late one.
Phase 3 — Step-down altitudes and MDA
1
Typical VOR step-down profile — how it works
Fix / PointAltitudeAutopilot Action
IAF3,000 ft MSLALT HOLD — maintain IAF altitude
Step-down fix 12,400 ft MSLDisengage ALT — descend — re-engage ALT at 2,400
FAF2,100 ft MSLDisengage ALT — descend — re-engage ALT at 2,100
MDAPer plateDisengage ALT — descend to MDA — re-engage ALT
MAPMDA or aboveVisual contact → land. No contact → go-around
You must re-engage ALT HOLD at each step-down altitude. If you forget, the aircraft will continue descending below the step-down altitude — a potential terrain collision in real life, and a grade failure in training.
2
Mandatory altitude callouts — IAF to MAP
  • At IAF — "Gear down, Flaps 17, VOR approach, descending"
  • At each step-down fix — "Step-down [altitude], leveling"
  • 1,000 ft AGL — "One thousand feet"
  • 500 ft AGL — "Five hundred, stable" or "Unstable — go-around"
  • At MDA — "Minimums, leveling — looking for runway"
  • Runway in sight — "Visual, landing"
  • At MAP, no visual — "Missed approach"
!
Instructor monitoring — VOR common errors
  • APR pressed → "APR is for ILS only — press NAV HOLD instead"
  • Wrong PFD mode → "Switch PFD softkey to NAV1 not GPS or ILS"
  • Skipped step-down → "You descended through [fix] altitude — re-engage ALT now"
  • MDA bust → "Re-engage ALT HOLD at MDA immediately"
  • Chasing VOR needle → "Stop chasing — hold heading and let the needle settle"
  • Station passage confusion → "That was station passage — hold heading until needle resettles"
3
MDA procedure — same as RNAV
  • Re-engage ALT HOLD at MDA — this is a hard floor
  • Continue inbound at MDA looking for the runway
  • Runway environment in sight before MAP → land
  • At MAP with no visual → go-around immediately
Never descend below MDA without the runway clearly in sight and a normal landing assured. This is a non-precision approach — the margin for error is smaller than ILS.
