RNAV / GPS Approach Guide

GPS-based approaches using the FMS flight plan for lateral guidance. No ILS frequency required. PFD softkey must be set to GPS. Vertical guidance managed manually to MDA.

Most important difference from ILS
RNAV uses GPS position — NOT a ground navaid. No frequency to tune in NAV page. The FMS FPLN flight plan drives lateral guidance.
  • No ILS frequency in FMS NAV page
  • PFD softkey set to GPS not ILS
  • Use NAV HOLD — never APR for RNAV
  • No automatic glideslope — manage descent manually
  • Level off at MDA — do not descend through it
Pressing APR mode for RNAV does nothing useful. APR needs an ILS signal. Use NAV HOLD instead.

Descent rate calculation

No glideslope needle exists for RNAV. Calculate required fpm before the FAF:

Groundspeedfpm for 3° descent

200 kt~1,000 fpm
Rule: Groundspeed × 5 = approximate fpm for 3° descent path.

Descent from FAF — no glideslope needle
  • Cross FAF → press ALT to disengage altitude hold
  • Set target vertical speed (GS × 5 fpm)
  • Monitor altitude vs distance continuously
  • At MDA: press ALT to re-engage — hold that altitude
No glideslope needle exists. You must actively monitor and manage your descent rate the entire time from FAF to MDA.
Phase 5 — Final and MDA
1
MDA procedure — level off and look
  • Level off at MDA — re-engage ALT HOLD
  • Continue inbound looking for runway environment
  • Runway in sight before MAP → land
  • At MAP no visual → immediate go-around
MDA is a hard floor. Do not descend below until over the runway threshold with landing assured.
!
Common student errors
  • APR instead of NAV → "Press NAV not APR for RNAV"
  • ILS PFD mode → "Check softkey — needs GPS not ILS"
  • MDA bust → "Re-engage ALT HOLD at MDA now"
  • Late descent → "You passed FAF — start descending"
  • No DIR used → "Use the DIR page for direct to final"
