Phase 2 & 3 Training Syllabus

Complete instructor guide covering all graded events for Phases 2 and 3. Each event includes graded items with standards, step-by-step training sequences, and evaluation criteria.

Syllabus overview

Phase 2 is conducted with the instructor in the seat providing active coaching and correction. Students are evaluated but receive immediate feedback on every graded item.

Phase 3 — Evaluation sortie (no coaching)

Phase 3 is a complete combat mission profile flown as a single integrated sortie. The instructor observes only. Intervention is limited to safety of flight situations.

#Event
3-1Independent SimBrief plan filed and briefed

Grading: Q = Qualified (standard met), U = Unqualified (standard not met). A U grade on any Phase 3 item requires re-accomplishment of the corresponding Phase 2 event before a re-check.

Phase 2 — Event 2-1
Takeoff, Climb, Pattern, Approach, and Landing
The student demonstrates safe takeoff technique, correct climb profile, traffic pattern procedures, approach speed control, accurate touchdown, and go-around capability.
!
Preflight brief checklist — instructor
  • Weather: VMC — ceiling ≥ 3,000 ft, visibility ≥ 5 SM
  • Runway in use: RWY 34L preferred. Brief departure and return routing
  • Fuel load: 50,000–60,000 lb (lighter weight for pattern work)
  • Brief go-around criteria: engine issues, unstable approach, ATC instruction
  • Confirm student knows pattern altitude (2,500 ft MSL) and circuit direction
  • Quiz student on VR and VREF from FMS PERF page before engine start
G
Graded items and standards
#Graded ItemStandardGrade
1Takeoff techniqueRotate at computed VR (approx 145–155 KIAS light weight). Smooth rotation to ~10° pitch. No runway excursions. Centerline ±10 ft.Q / U
2Climb profileGear up by 300 ft AGL. Climb 250 KIAS on departure heading ±5°. Above 10,000 ft: accelerate to 300 KIAS. Altitude capture ±100 ft.Q / U
3Pattern altitudeDownwind at 2,500 ft MSL ±100 ft. Speed 250 KIAS on downwind, decelerating on base.Q / U
4Approach speedFinal at VREF ±5 KIAS. Speed stable by 500 ft AGL.Q / U
5Landing touchdown zoneTouchdown within first 2,000 ft of runway. Centerline ±15 ft. No porpoising or bouncing.Q / U
6Go-around executionImmediate power advance to MIL. Positive climb established. Gear up at positive rate. Correct missed approach heading and altitude.Q / U
S
Step-by-step training sequence
#Instructor action / cueExpected student response
1BRIEF: Review V-speeds, runway length, and weight. Quiz student on VR and VREF from FMS PERF page.Student recites correct speeds from KwikFlight performance page without prompting.
2PRE-TAKEOFF: Confirm throttle detents understood. Instruct student to advance to MIL and hold brakes until engines stable.Student holds brakes, confirms engines stable, then releases brakes smoothly.
3TAKEOFF ROLL: Monitor centerline tracking. Call "Rotate" at correct VR speed.Student rotates smoothly to ~10° pitch attitude. No over-rotation.
4INITIAL CLIMB: At positive rate of climb call "Gear Up." Monitor pitch and heading.Student selects gear up, maintains climb heading ±5°, targets 250 KIAS.
5PATTERN: Call turning crosswind at 500 ft AGL, downwind at pattern altitude. Prompt speed reduction.Student rolls out on downwind heading, levels at 2,500 ft MSL, decelerates.
6BASE / FINAL: Call "Gear Down, Flaps 17" at correct gate. Monitor speed on final.Student configures aircraft, achieves VREF on glide path, calls "Stable."
7FLARE: Coach flare callout "Flare — hold it." Monitor sink rate and aim point.Student arrests sink rate, touches down in zone, applies aero-braking.
8GO-AROUND (surprise): Call "Go Around" without warning at any point on approach.Student applies power immediately, pitches positive, calls "Go Around," climbs safely.
9DEBRIEF: Grade each item. Annotate deviations. Assign follow-on training if U grades.Student participates in critique, acknowledges corrections, notes improvement areas.
Phase 2 — Event 2-2
ILS / Instrument Approach, Missed Approach, and Hold
The student demonstrates precision instrument approaches to DH, executes the missed approach and hold entry as published for KDYS ILS RWY 34L.
!
Preflight brief checklist
  • Set weather: ceiling 300–800 ft OVC, visibility 1–2 SM (IMC conditions)
  • Load KDYS ILS 34L plate. Brief all fixes, DH (200 ft HAT), missed approach heading and altitude
  • Confirm ILS tuned in FMS NAV page VOR1 (freq 110.3) and identifier I-DYS shows
  • Brief hold entry geometry using published hold at KDYS missed approach fix
  • Confirm student knows PFD softkey must be ILS — not GPS
G
Graded items and standards
#Graded ItemStandardGrade
1Intercept geometryLocalizer captured from intercept angle ≤30°. No S-turns or overshoots beyond full-scale deflection.Q / U
2Glide slope / course trackingWithin ±½ dot localizer AND ±½ dot glideslope from FAF to DH. Corrections smooth and timely.Q / U
3Decision height executionAt DH: if runway NOT in sight, immediate go-around initiated. No descent below DH without visual. Call "Minimums" at DH.Q / U
4Missed approach procedurePublished missed approach executed precisely: correct heading, climb to published altitude, fly to published fix. No delay in power application.Q / U
5Hold entryCorrect entry type (direct / parallel / teardrop) determined accurately. Abeam timing correct (1 min standard). Inbound rollout on course ±5°.Q / U
S
Step-by-step training sequence
#Instructor action / cueExpected student response
1BRIEF: Quiz student on DH, missed approach heading, and hold entry type before vectors.Student correctly identifies DH (200 ft HAT), MA heading, and hold parameters.
2SETUP: Position aircraft on vectors for ILS. Instruct student to configure FMS NAV page (110.3 / I-DYS / CRS 359) and set PFD to ILS mode.Student tunes ILS, confirms identifier I-DYS, sets CRS 359, switches PFD to ILS softkey.
3VECTORS: Clear student to intercept localizer. Confirm APR is armed (orange on PFD). Monitor approach track.Student arms APR mode, intercepts within ±30° track angle, localizer captures cleanly.
4GLIDESLOPE ALIVE: Call "Glideslope alive." Aircraft in APR mode should capture automatically.ALT HOLD changes to G/S on PFD. Aircraft follows 3° descent path.
5FAF TO DH: Monitor deviations. Call out any deviation exceeding ½ dot.Student corrects inside ½ dot. Calls altitudes at FAF, 1,000, 500, and 100 ft.
6MINIMUMS: Call "Runway in sight" or "Not in sight" as appropriate for the scenario.If not in sight: immediate go-around. If in sight: student calls "Landing" and continues.
7MISSED APPROACH: Issue "Execute missed approach" at any point during approach without warning.Student immediately applies power, pitches for climb, calls "Go Around" on radio.
8HOLD: Vector student to published hold. Quiz hold entry type before arriving at fix.Student correctly identifies entry type, flies hold within timing standards, ±5° inbound.
Phase 2 — Event 2-3
Aerial Refueling (AR)
The student rendezvous with the AI tanker, establishes pre-contact position, makes receptacle contact, holds position during fuel transfer, and executes a safe breakaway.
!
Preflight brief checklist
  • Spawn AI tanker (KC-135 or KC-10) ahead of student at AR track altitude and speed
  • Brief AR track: altitude FL200–FL230, airspeed 270–290 KIAS
  • Confirm receptacle door is open on overhead panel (AERIAL REFUEL LT switch)
  • Brief pre-contact position: 50 ft directly behind boom, slightly below
  • Brief breakaway procedure: pilot signal or emergency — immediate descent / right turn
BOOM LIMITS: Do not exceed ±10 ft lateral or 6 ft vertical in contact. Excessive movement risks boom disconnect. Brief student on micro-correction technique before flying the event.
G
Graded items and standards
#Graded ItemStandardGrade
1Tanker rendezvousVisual contact established. Closes at correct overtake speed (max 5 kt) from 1 NM to observation position. No unsafe closure rates.Q / U
2Pre-contact positionStable 50 ft aft, slightly below boom tip, within ±10 ft of centerline for ≥15 seconds before contact attempt.Q / U
3Boom / receptacle contact techniqueSmooth power application to drive into contact. Receptacle engages on first or second attempt. No abrupt pitch or roll inputs.Q / U
4Position hold stabilityMaintains contact for 60 seconds with ≤2 inadvertent disconnects. Lateral drift ≤±5 ft, vertical ≤±3 ft during sustained contact.Q / U
5Breakaway procedureOn "Breakaway" call: immediate power reduction, descend 500 ft, turn right 30°. No hesitation. Clear of tanker wake within 10 seconds.Q / U
S
Step-by-step training sequence
#Instructor action / cueExpected student response
1SETUP: Position aircraft 5 NM directly behind tanker at same altitude. Brief tanker parameters.Student visually acquires tanker, confirms tanker lights and markings.
2RENDEZVOUS: Instruct closure to observation position (2 o'clock, 1,000 ft, same altitude).Student controls overtake, arrives at observation with no excessive speed.
3OBSERVATION: Student checks tanker ready (green pre-contact light). Move to pre-contact.Student transitions smoothly to 50 ft aft, centers on boom.
4PRE-CONTACT HOLD: Call "Stabilize pre-contact." Time 15-second stable hold.Student maintains position. Minor corrections only. No hunting or oscillating.
5CONTACT: Instruct student to "Drive forward to contact."Student applies small power increase, receptacle engages boom. Calls "Contact."
6FUEL TRANSFER: Monitor position for 60 seconds. Call out drifts over ±5 ft.Student maintains contact, makes micro-corrections with throttle and stick inputs.
7BREAKAWAY: Issue "Breakaway, breakaway, breakaway" call without warning.Student reduces power, descends 500 ft, turns right 30°, clears tanker zone.
Phase 2 — Event 2-4
Cross-Country IFR — KDYS → KTIK → KDYS
Student plans and flies a complete IFR cross-country from KDYS to KTIK (Tinker AFB, OK), executes touch-and-go landings at KTIK, and recovers IFR to KDYS.
G
Graded items and standards
#Graded ItemStandardGrade
1SimBrief plan qualityFiled route is realistic with SID/STAR where applicable. Fuel adequate with reserves. Alternate filed if required. OFP reviewed verbally in brief.Q / U
2IFR route complianceFlies filed route without deviation except with ATC clearance. All altitude crossing restrictions met within ±200 ft.Q / U
3ATC communicationReadbacks correct and complete. Position reports on time. Requests phrased professionally. No unanswered ATC calls.Q / U
4T&G technique at KTIKApproach stabilized at VREF ±5 kt. Touchdown in zone. Immediate go-around execution on rollout. Pattern maintained at KTIK altitude.Q / U
5Final IFR approach at KDYSILS 34L tracked within ±½ dot to DH. Landing touchdown in zone. Full stop. Taxi to parking without errors.Q / U
Phase 2 — Event 2-5
Low-Level / Time-On-Target (TOT)
Student flies a briefed low-level route at 500 ft AGL, maintaining speed and altitude, hitting a designated TOT within ±30 seconds, executing planned turns, and correctly recognizing abort criteria.
G
Graded items and standards
#Graded ItemStandardGrade
1Altitude maintenanceMaintains 500 ft AGL ±100 ft throughout route. No sustained deviation below 400 ft or above 700 ft AGL.Q / U
2Speed disciplineMaintains planned low-level airspeed ±10 KIAS. Speed stable between turn points.Q / U
3TOT accuracyArrives at target within ±30 seconds of planned TOT. Speed adjustments smooth — no large corrections inside 1 NM of target.Q / U
4Turn techniqueBank angle ≤60° at low level. Roll-in at planned turn point ±0.3 NM. Rollout on exit heading ±5°.Q / U
5Abort criteria recognitionCorrectly identifies and calls abort when instructor announces a simulated abort condition. Immediate climb to safe altitude initiated without hesitation.Q / U
S
Step-by-step training sequence
#Instructor action / cueExpected student response
1BRIEF: Review route card, TOT, and abort criteria. Student recites all TPs from memory.Student briefs route: entry, all TPs with headings, TOT time, exit.
2ENTRY: Confirm aircraft on course at entry point at correct altitude and speed.Student rolls out on course, descends to 500 ft AGL, stabilizes speed.
3TP 1: Count down "TP 1 in 5, 4, 3…" to aid timing. Monitor bank angle.Student initiates bank ≤60°, rollout on new heading ±5°.
4SPEED CHECK: Midpoint — ask "TOT check — are you early or late?"Student computes timing, adjusts speed to hit TOT (±10 KIAS correction).
5ABORT: Announce surprise abort condition (e.g. "Weather below minimums at target").Student immediately calls "Abort, Abort," climbs to 2,000 ft AGL.
6TARGET: Call "TOT — Time now" at planned TOT time.Student announces TOT arrival. Instructor records actual vs planned time delta.
Phase 2 — Event 2-6
Weapons Employment
Student loads correct target coordinates into the KwikFlight weapons system, meets all delivery parameters, executes safe escape maneuver, and assesses weapon impact.
G
Graded items and standards
#Graded ItemStandardGrade
1Target coordinates loaded correctlyStudent enters target lat/long exactly as assigned. Verified on MFD before IP. No digit transpositions. Weapon type correct.Q / U
2Delivery parameters metAirspeed within ±10 KIAS of planned release speed. Altitude within ±200 ft. Heading within ±3° at release.Q / U
3Safe escapePost-release: immediate climbing turn to safe escape altitude. Exit heading maintained. No extended level flight after release.Q / U
4Weapon impact assessmentStudent calls "Weapons away" at release, "Splash" at impact, reports estimated CEP. Demonstrates understanding of on-target vs off-target result.Q / U
3
Evaluation sortie — no coaching
Phase 3 is a complete combat mission profile flown as a single integrated sortie. The instructor observes and grades without routine coaching. Intervention is limited to safety-of-flight situations only. A U grade on any item requires re-accomplishment of the associated Phase 2 event before re-check.
